Martin Buber's Insight

I–It

Relating to others as objects, roles, or data points
I–Thou

Relating to others as whole beings, with mutual presence
"All real living is meeting."
→

Why It Matters for Customer Discovery

1
Changes what you see
Whole human, not just segment
2
Changes what they share
Deeper trust, richer stories
3
Reduces projection bias
See their world, not your assumptions
4
Connects ethics and outcomes
Better relationships, better adoption
→

The Breakthrough

• Predetermined questions
• Surface-level responses
• Confirmation bias

After: Open Conversation

• Real context revealed

Outcome: Changed the product direction; adoption soared

Coffee Shop Observation Analogy

☕👀

Barista noticing customer's hesitation at menu

I–Thou barista saw HOW you engaged, not just WHAT you ordered
Same principle applies to customer discovery
→

Bringing I–Thou Into Practice

Before

Drop "validate idea"
→
"be changed by learning"

During

• Listen deeply
• Follow emotional cues
• Observe environment

After

Reflect on WHO you met, not just WHAT you learned

→

Practical Tips for Presence

👁️‍🗨️
👁️
Eye contact, no devices
Full presence in the moment
❓
Open-ended prompts
"Tell me about the last time..."
🧭
Follow curiosity, not just script
Let genuine interest guide the conversation
→

The Payoff

I–Thou turns interviews into relationships
Observation transforms assumptions into truths
Real empathy → better solutions → lasting loyalty
→

Call to Action

In your next customer interaction:

1
Be present
Give them your full attention
2
See the whole person
Not just a data point or segment
3
Let them change your mind
Be open to unexpected insights
